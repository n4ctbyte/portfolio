Serif Fonts

Serif fonts have small lines or strokes attached to the end of larger strokes in a letter. They're often associated with tradition, reliability, and formality.

Sans Serif Fonts

Sans serif fonts lack the small projecting features called "serifs" at the end of strokes. They tend to have a cleaner, more modern look.
